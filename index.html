BBS. Bulletin Boards & Social Media

— P2020174 Tzelepis Fotios

Computer bulletin boards, or BBSs, are electronic message boards that allow users to post messages, share files, and engage in discussions on various topics. These were popular in the 1980s and 1990s, but have since been largely replaced by social media platforms. However, there are sitll some die-hard BBS users who prefer the simplicity and privacy of these forums.

Social Media platforms such as Facebook, Twitter, and LinkedIn began to emerge in the midd-2000s, offering users new ways to connect and interact with each other. These platforms enabled users to share information, media, and messages in real-time, which facilitated more dynamic and immediate communication. Today, social media platforms and messaging apps dominate online communication and have become integral parts of many people's daily lives."
